# Treatment Guide

> **Scope**: Per-plant watering, fertilizing, pruning, and pest/disease protocols for common urban garden crops.
> **Load when**: prescribing care routines, diagnosing plant problems, or building treatment tabs in the workbook.

---

## Watering Guidelines

### General Principles

| Principle | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Check soil first | Stick finger 2cm into soil. Water only when dry at that depth (most crops). |
| Morning watering | Water at base in morning. Wet foliage + heat = fungal disease. |
| Container frequency | Containers dry out 2–3× faster than ground beds. Check daily in summer. |
| Consistency | Inconsistent watering causes blossom end rot (tomato, pepper) and tip burn (lettuce). |
| Drain holes | Containers must drain. Standing water = root rot. |

### Watering Frequency by Plant (summer, warm conditions)

| Plant | Frequency | Method | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Tomato | Every 1–2 days (container); every 2–3 days (ground) | Base watering | Never let wilt; consistent moisture prevents BER |
| Pepper | Every 2–3 days | Base watering | More drought-tolerant than tomato; avoid waterlogging |
| Eggplant | Every 2–3 days | Base watering | Consistent moisture for uniform fruit |
| Cucumber | Daily in heat | Base watering | Very thirsty; mulch to retain moisture |
| Zucchini/squash | Every 2–3 days | Base watering | Large leaves lose moisture fast |
| Lettuce | Every 1–2 days | Gentle overhead or base | Shallow roots; keep consistently moist |
| Spinach/kale | Every 2 days | Base watering | Wilts fast; mulch helps |
| Herbs (basil, cilantro, dill) | Every 1–2 days | Base watering | Basil especially sensitive to drought |
| Herbs (rosemary, thyme, sage) | Every 3–5 days | Base watering | Drought-tolerant; overwatering kills them |
| Strawberry | Every 1–2 days (fruiting) | Drip or base | Keep fruit dry to prevent mold |
| Beans/peas | Every 2–3 days | Base watering | Extra water at flowering and pod fill |

## Fertilizing Schedules

### NPK Quick Reference

| Nutrient | Symbol | Role | When to emphasize |
|----------|--------|------|-------------------|
| Nitrogen | N | Leaf and stem growth | Seedlings, leafy greens |
| Phosphorus | P | Root and flower development | Transplanting, flowering |
| Potassium | K | Fruit quality, disease resistance | Fruiting stage |

### Fertilizing by Growth Stage

| Stage | Fertilizer Type | Application |
|-------|----------------|-------------|
| Seed starting | Starter mix only (no fertilizer) | None until first true leaves |
| Seedling (first true leaves) | Half-strength balanced liquid (10-10-10 or similar) | Every 2 weeks |
| Transplant | Slow-release granular mixed into soil | Once at transplanting |
| Vegetative growth | Balanced NPK (10-10-10 or 7-7-7) | Every 2 weeks liquid OR monthly granular |
| Flowering and fruiting | Low-N, higher P/K (e.g., 4-4.5-8 tomato formula, or 5-10-10) | Every 1–2 weeks |
| Harvest maintenance | Continued P/K focus | Every 2 weeks until season end |

### Per-Plant Fertilizer Protocols

| Plant | Frequency | Product Type | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Tomato | Every 1–2 weeks from flowering | Tomato-specific (e.g., NPK ~4-4.5-8) | Calcium supplement if BER; avoid excess N (too leafy, little fruit) |
| Pepper | Every 2 weeks | Balanced early; K-focused at flowering | Similar to tomato; heavy feeder |
| Eggplant | Every 2 weeks | Balanced; tomato feed at fruit set | Feed consistently |
| Cucumber | Every 1–2 weeks | Balanced (10-10-10 type) | Heavy feeder; consistent feeding = more fruit |
| Zucchini/squash | Every 2 weeks | Balanced | Modest feeder vs. cucumber |
| Lettuce, spinach | Every 2–3 weeks | High-N liquid (e.g., 10-5-5) | Leafy crops want nitrogen |
| Kale, chard | Every 3 weeks | Balanced | Moderate feeders |
| Brassicas | Every 3 weeks | Balanced | Avoid excess N late season |
| Beans/peas | Minimal | Minimal or none | Fix own nitrogen; excess N = leaves, no pods |
| Herbs (basil, parsley) | Every 2–3 weeks | Half-strength balanced | Monthly is sufficient for most herbs |
| Herbs (rosemary, thyme) | Monthly | Half-strength balanced | Light feeders; overfeeding reduces flavor oils |
| Strawberry | Every 2 weeks (fruiting) | High-K liquid | Balanced post-harvest to build next year's crowns |

## Pruning and Training

| Plant | Technique | When | Why |
|-------|-----------|------|-----|
| Tomato (indeterminate) | Remove suckers (side shoots in leaf axils); maintain 1–2 main stems | Weekly from transplant | Controls size; improves air circulation; speeds fruit |
| Tomato (determinate) | Minimal pruning; remove only diseased leaves | As needed | Determinate sets all fruit at once; heavy pruning reduces yield |
| Pepper | Pinch growing tip after 6–8 leaf pairs; remove first flower bud | At transplant | Encourages bushy growth and delayed but heavier fruiting |
| Cucumber | Train up trellis; remove suckers to 1 main stem | Weekly | Vertical growing saves space; improves air circulation |
| Basil | Pinch flower buds as soon as they appear | Every 1–2 weeks | Prevents bolting; keeps leaves productive for 2–3 months longer |
| Strawberry | Remove runners unless propagating | As they appear | Runners steal energy from fruiting |
| Beans (pole) | Train up support; no pruning needed | At planting | Wind tendrils onto support at start |
| Peas | Train up trellis; pinch growing tip at desired height | Weekly | Controls height; encourages bushing |

## Common Pests

### Pest Identification and Response

| Pest | Affected Plants | Signs | Organic Response | Conventional Response |
|------|----------------|-------|------------------|-----------------------|
| Aphids | Most crops, especially tomato, pepper, kale, beans | Clusters of small green/black/white insects on new growth; sticky residue | Blast with water; insecticidal soap spray (5ml/L); release ladybugs | Pyrethrin spray |
| Spider mites | Tomato, cucumber, pepper, eggplant | Fine webbing under leaves; stippled/yellowing foliage; tiny red/brown dots | Increase humidity; insecticidal soap; neem oil (2ml/L + 1ml dish soap) | Miticide |
| Whitefly | Tomato, pepper, brassicas | Clouds of white insects when plant disturbed; sticky honeydew | Yellow sticky traps; insecticidal soap; reflective mulch | Pyrethrin |
| Fungus gnats | Seedlings / any container | Tiny black flies near soil; larvae in soil damage roots | Allow soil to dry between waterings; sticky yellow traps; BTi (Bacillus thuringiensis israelensis) drench | Imidacloprid soil drench |
| Slugs/snails | Lettuce, seedlings, strawberry | Irregular holes in leaves; slime trails | Copper tape around pots; beer traps; diatomaceous earth ring | Iron phosphate bait (Ferramol) |
| Caterpillars | Brassicas (cabbage white), tomato (hornworm) | Holes in leaves; droppings | Hand-pick; Bt spray (Bacillus thuringiensis kurstaki) | Spinosad |
| Cutworms | Seedlings (stems cut at soil level) | Seedlings toppled; stem cut at base | Collar around stem (toilet roll); diatomaceous earth | Chlorpyrifos (last resort) |
| Scale | Citrus, fig | Brown bumps on stems; sticky leaves | Scrape off; isopropyl alcohol on cotton; neem oil | Systemic insecticide |
| Vine weevil | Strawberry, container plants | Notched leaf edges (adult); larvae eat roots | Nematodes (Steinernema kraussei) — effective in Netherlands climate | Imidacloprid |

## Common Diseases

| Disease | Affected Plants | Signs | Prevention | Treatment |
|---------|----------------|-------|------------|-----------|
| Blossom end rot | Tomato, pepper, eggplant, zucchini | Dark leathery patch on blossom end of fruit | Consistent watering; calcium supplement | Foliar calcium spray; correct watering |
| Powdery mildew | Zucchini, cucumber, beans, peas | White powdery coating on leaves | Good air circulation; avoid wetting foliage | Baking soda spray (5g/L + dish soap); potassium bicarbonate; neem oil |
| Damping off | Seedlings | Stem rotting at soil line; seedling collapses | Sterile seed mix; do not overwater; good air flow | Remove affected; improve drainage; chamomile tea drench |
| Early blight | Tomato | Dark spots with yellow halo on lower leaves; moves upward | Mulch; avoid wetting foliage; remove lower leaves | Copper fungicide spray; remove affected leaves |
| Late blight | Tomato, potato | Water-soaked grey-brown patches; white mold underside; spreads rapidly in wet weather | Resistant varieties; good air flow; avoid wetting leaves | Copper fungicide at first sign; remove severely affected |
| Grey mould (Botrytis) | Strawberry, tomato, lettuce | Grey fuzzy mould on fruit or stems | Air circulation; remove dead material; avoid overhead watering | Remove affected parts; reduce humidity; bordeaux mixture |
| Root rot | Any container plant | Wilting despite moist soil; brown slimy roots | Drainage holes; do not overwater | Repot with fresh dry soil; trim rotten roots; treat with hydrogen peroxide (3% diluted 1:4) |

## Organic Spray Recipes

| Spray | Ingredients | Use |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Insecticidal soap | 5ml pure liquid soap (not detergent) per 1L water | Aphids, whitefly, spider mites |
| Neem oil spray | 2ml neem oil + 1ml dish soap per 1L warm water | Fungal, mites, aphids, general pest |
| Baking soda fungicide | 5g baking soda + 2ml dish soap per 1L water | Powdery mildew |
| Garlic spray | 6 cloves blended + strained + 1L water | General pest deterrent |
| Chilli spray | 10g chilli powder + 1L water + drop dish soap | Aphids, caterpillars, slugs |

Apply all sprays in the evening to avoid leaf burn. Test on small area first.
